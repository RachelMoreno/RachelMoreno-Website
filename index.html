My name is Rachel T. Moreno. I am a Riverside, California local. I was raised by a single mother of three with a limited high school level education. As a result, the value of education was embedded into my mind from a young age. My high school years were spent in the halls of John W. North High School, where I graduated in 2018. During those four years, I was involved in various clubs and organizations, including United Student League, soccer, and M.E.Ch.A. My high school experiences taught me the value of time, discipline, and the sense of community.

My hard work, time, and efforts in high school paid off as it created a path that led me to the University of California, Riverside. I applied and was accepted to UCR as a Philosophy major in the College of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences, also known as CHASS. I am currently in the beginning of my second year at UCR. Currently, I am involved in the First Gen Student Association that creates a community for first generation students that do not have the same resources and support systems that their classmates do. I am always looking to become more involved, and I am hoping to expand my list of involvement on campus soon.

When I am not on campus, I am at my job, where I work full-time hours. I work in a small family owned restaurant in Riverside, California, named D'Elia's Grinders. My job consists of working the register, where I interact with customers and develop my social skills, making grinders, and cleaning, where I am expected to maintain a safe and clean environment for myself, my coworkers, and the customers. This job taught me problem solving, communication, and time manage skills in a laborous manner that greatly contrasts the education environment. It has presented new challenges to me that I never expected from a minimum wage job.
